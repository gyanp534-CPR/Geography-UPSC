Q.4 Climatology Medium PYQ · 2019
Consider the following statements about the Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD):

1. A positive IOD is associated with above-normal monsoon rainfall over India.
2. IOD is measured by the difference in Sea Surface Temperatures between the western and eastern Indian Ocean.
3. A negative IOD event was observed in 2019, contributing to severe drought conditions over India.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
A1 only
B2 and 3 only
C1 and 2 only
D1, 2 and 3
Explanation: Statements 1 & 2 are correct. A positive IOD (warmer western Indian Ocean) enhances moisture-laden winds toward India, boosting monsoon. The IOD index is indeed defined by SST difference across the Indian Ocean. Statement 3 is incorrect — 2019 actually saw a positive IOD, which partially offset El Niño effects and supported a reasonable monsoon season.

Syllabus links: Cyclogenesis → Tropical Cyclones → Arabian Sea vs Bay of Bengal
June 2024 · 3 concepts tagged
Geographic significance: Cyclone Biparjoy developed in the Arabian Sea — unusual for its intensity at that latitude. Key UPSC angle: warm SSTs (>26°C) required for cyclogenesis, Coriolis force role, landfall in ecologically sensitive Kutch/Rann area.

Syllabus connect: Physical Geography → Climatology → Tropical cyclones. Also connects to GS3 — disaster management, Early Warning Systems.

PYQ pattern: UPSC 2020 asked about Bay of Bengal cyclone frequency vs Arabian Sea. This event gives fresh data points for answer enrichment.

Syllabus links: Himalayan rivers → Braided channels → Flood plain dynamics
July 2024 · 5 concepts tagged
Geographic significance: Brahmaputra carries one of world's highest sediment loads. Flash floods originate in tectonically unstable Arunachal hills; sedimentation raises riverbed annually. UPSC angle: why Brahmaputra floods every year, relationship to Himalayan glacial melt + monsoon pattern.

Multidimensional angle: GS1 (rivers) + GS3 (disaster management) + GS2 (centre-state relations for flood funds). Rich answer source.
